About Me

Hello! My name is Nicholas Farkash. I am an undergraduate student at CUNY Queens College. This website features projects that I have worked on throughout my CS journey. The purpose of this website is not only to share my work, but also to serve as a portfolio, both for personal and professional purposes.

My experience in programming began when I was a sophomore in high school. Having a passion for science, I approached the head of the science department and inquired about the SUNY Albany Affiliated High School Science Research Program that some of my classmates were in. I wasn't sure what topic I wanted to research at first, but I eventually settled on Video Game Development. Over the next two years, I utilized C# and Unity to create two very simple video game demos - a 2D Platformer and a top-down RPG. Unfortunately, both of these games were lost in a hard drive failure, and all that remains is what I included in my Science Research presentations. Regardless, I thoroughly enjoyed working on both projects and my passion for programming grew.

When the time came to apply to college, I knew I would be attending Stony Brook University, but I didn't know what I would be studying. While working on these games, I was simultaneously growing interested in the field of physics. After conversing with many of my high school science teachers, I applied to Stony Brook University as a Physics major, but after two years, I realized that my true passion was in computer science. Due to the extreme influx of prospective CS students at Stony Brook, I was unable to transfer into their program. After looking around at other schools, I settled on CUNY Queens College, where I am continuing to further my studies.
