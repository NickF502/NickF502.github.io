Science Research (2018-2020)

Main character from the first game (2D Platformer)

In the summer leading into my junior year of high school, I decided to join my school's Science Research in the High School program, which is a STEM program sponsored by the University at Albany. I wasn't sure what topic I wanted to reseatch, but I always had an interest in the STEM field. I had a wide array of personal intersts, ranging from black holes to nanorobotics, and I could choose whatever I wanted. Ultimately I decided on video game development, and I'm confident that I ended up choosing perfectly.

The program was rigorous, as it required a lot of time spent outside of class finding and reading peer reviewed articles, learning how to code (I had no experience going into these projects), and creating a posterboard and presentation. Despite all of this work, I had a lot of fun sitting in the research room during study hall and lunch periods talking with peers as I worked. I made many friends (one of which I consider a brother to this day), received a new perspective on the field of computer science, and learned a lot from my peers. I credit this program with fueling my passion for STEM and keeping me motivated to continue learning.

Featured below are each of the two projects that I created while researching video game development. Unfortunately, both projects were destroyed in a hard drive failure on my old laptop, and I failed to store a backup of them anywhere since I didn't intend on pursuing a future in video game development at the time. This is one of the biggest regrets of my life, as I put a lot of time into these projects and thoroughly enjoyed my time working on them. Although the games no longer exist, some fragments of them live on through various screenshots and GIFs I included in my symposium presentations. Had I not randomly decided to log into my old high school Google Drive to take a look around, all of the work I did over those two years may have been completely forgotten. This page contains what remains of my projects.

Main character from the second game (RPG)

Junior Year - 2D Platformer Game

The game I worked on for my junior year was a basic 2D platformer. I followed alongside YouTube videos to learn how to do pretty much everything. (Credit to Brackeys for guiding me through the process and providing valuable resources for the process) Having no background in computer science whatsoever, I knew that I wouldn't be able to create a game from scratch, but external resources allowed me to take my first steps towards creating my own video game. The game itself was very straightforward. The player controls a knight character and tries to navigate a level and reach the end. I ended up creating two levels: the first was a practice level that I used to teach myself how to build levels and the second was the level I showcased during symposium.
